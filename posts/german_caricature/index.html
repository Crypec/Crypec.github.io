Caricature No. 2

Samuel Jonasens caricature shows a a black guy, with a rope around his neck, getting dragged along the ground by a truck with the lettering “Police Brutality”, while simulataniously holding up a sign with the lettering “GUILTY ONLY OF BEING BLACK”. The ground is made up of a newsletter of the quite fitting name the “daily bluff”, while the prominent first side headline is “Liberty and justice for all… What a cruel hoax”. One particular obvious symbol is of course the getting dragged behind a horse until dead, this was actually a common punishment for runaway slaves in the wild west. This carricature highlights the reality of police brutality against people of color and minorities in America. This of course is constrasted by the idea of the american dream, after which every american is given the right to life, liberty and the pursuit of happyness. According to a recent study by Harvard, black people are six times more likely to be killed by the police. But other studies have shown no correlation for crimerate or violence if adjusted to socioeconomic factors. This means we should be focusing more on improving peoples lives, training police officers in non violent deescalation techniques and not arming the police with more and more weapons.
